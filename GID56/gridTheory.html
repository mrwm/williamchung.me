Alum Rock Park
 

Alum Rock park is like a hidden gem of San Jose. With history dating back to 1872*, it is the oldest municipal park in California. Up until the 1960's, the park used to be a place to enjoy spas, with an indoor swimming pool, restaurant, and other recreation facilities. Despite closing majority of the park recreation facilities for restoration of the surrounding nature, Alum Rock Park still retains parts of old structures such as the rail road, spas, and a few buildings.

 
More photos of Alum Rock Park

This park spans over 720 acres of land* and holds over 13 miles of trails where a couple are usable for horses and bikers. It's trails meander through the slopes of San Jose's foothills. Within the park are a bunch of plants and animals such as deers, rabbits, quails, and a bunch of squirrels. On the other hand, the creek within the park (Penitencia Creek) is filled with animals such as small fishies, frogs and tadoles, ducklings and ducks, and small critters such as water striders. The park is still popular for recreational activities such as outdoor birthday parties, barbecues, hiking, horseback riding, and much more.
